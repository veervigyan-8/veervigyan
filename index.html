Physics

Newton's Laws of Motion

Foundation of Classical Mechanics

🔭

First Law (Inertia): An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion, unless acted upon by a net external force. This is why passengers jerk forward when a bus brakes suddenly.

Second Law (F = ma): Net force = mass × acceleration. Doubling force → doubles acceleration. Doubling mass → halves acceleration (for the same force).

Third Law (Action–Reaction): For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction. A rocket expels hot gas downward → gas pushes rocket upward.

📌 Key Formulae

F = ma  |  p = mv  |  Impulse J = FΔt = Δp

📌 Important Applications

  • Seat belts reduce injury by increasing deceleration time → smaller impact force (impulse-momentum theorem).
  • A cricket batsman draws the bat back to increase contact time → reducing average force on hands.
  • Astronauts in orbit are in continuous free fall — hence they feel weightless.

📌 Common Exam Mistakes

  • Newton's 3rd Law pairs act on different objects — they never cancel each other out.
  • F = ma uses the net (resultant) force, not just one individual force.
  • Inertia is NOT a force — it is the property of matter to resist change in motion.
Physics

Electrostatics

Charges, Fields & Potential

⚡

Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r². The force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of their charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance. k = 9×10⁹ Nm²/C².

Electric Field (E): Force per unit positive test charge at a point. E = F/q₀ = kQ/r². Field lines go from +ve to −ve charges. E is a vector quantity.

Electric Potential (V): Work done per unit charge to bring a +ve charge from infinity to that point. V = kQ/r. It is a scalar — has no direction.

📌 Key Relations

E = −dV/dr  |  V = kQ/r  |  U = kq₁q₂/r

📌 Gauss's Law

  • Total electric flux through any closed surface = Q_enclosed / ε₀.
  • Useful for highly symmetric charge distributions (sphere, cylinder, plane).
  • Inside a conductor in electrostatic equilibrium: E = 0, entire surface is equipotential.

📌 Capacitors

  • C = Q/V. For a parallel plate capacitor: C = ε₀A/d.
  • Series: 1/C_total = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂. Parallel: C_total = C₁ + C₂.
  • Energy stored: U = ½CV² = Q²/2C.
Physics

Ray Optics

Reflection, Refraction & Lenses

🔬

Laws of Reflection: (1) Angle of incidence = angle of reflection (both from the normal). (2) Incident ray, reflected ray & normal are coplanar. Applies to plane and curved mirrors.

Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂. Light bends towards the normal when entering a denser medium (higher n). Refractive index n = c/v.

Lens Formula: 1/v − 1/u = 1/f. Convex lens (f +ve) converges light. Concave lens (f −ve) diverges. Power P = 1/f(m), unit: Dioptre (D).

📌 Mirror Formula & Sign Convention

1/v + 1/u = 1/f  |  m = −v/u  |  f = R/2
  • All distances measured from the pole (mirror) or optical centre (lens).
  • Direction of incident light = +ve direction. Opposite = −ve.

📌 Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

  • Occurs when light goes from denser to rarer medium & angle of incidence > critical angle (θc).
  • sin θc = n₂/n₁. For glass-air: θc ≈ 42°.
  • Applications: Optical fibres (internet cables), diamonds (brilliance), mirages, endoscopes.
Chemistry

Periodic Table & Trends

Foundation of Inorganic Chemistry

🗂️

Modern Periodic Law: Properties of elements are a periodic function of their atomic numbers. 18 groups (columns) and 7 periods (rows). Elements in same group have similar valence electron configurations.

Atomic Radius: Decreases across a period (increasing nuclear charge pulls electrons closer). Increases down a group (new electron shells added).

Ionization Enthalpy (IE): Energy needed to remove outermost electron from a gaseous atom. IE increases across period, decreases down group. Exception: IE of O < N (half-filled 2p³ is stable).

📌 Trend Summary Table

  • Electronegativity: ↑ across period, ↓ down group. Highest element: Fluorine (F = 4.0 Pauling scale).
  • Electron Affinity: Cl has higher EA than F despite F being more electronegative (F's small size → electron repulsion).
  • Metallic Character: ↑ down group, ↓ across period (left side = metals, right = non-metals).
  • Diagonal Relationship: Li≈Mg, Be≈Al, B≈Si — similar atomic/ionic radius ratio causes similar chemical behaviour.
Across period →: radius↓, IE↑, EN↑
Down group ↓: radius↑, IE↓, EN↓
